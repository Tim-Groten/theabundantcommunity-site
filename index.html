The Problem

Youth aging out of foster care often face homelessness, isolation, and limited access to networks that open doors. These barriers are heavy alone, and light together.

11–36%

Experience homelessness during transition to adulthood, AJPH

31–36%

Report at least one episode by age 26, Courtney et al.

~19k–24k

Age out annually in the U.S., AECF, PMC

We mobilize volunteers and social capital to change outcomes

Current Focus

We are building an abundant community through three partner groups, referral partners connect us with youth exiting care, training partners equip volunteers for complex needs, community partners bring people, relationships, and opportunity.

  • Referral partners, organizations in close contact with youth aging out
  • Training partners, for us, The Open Table, a research-backed model
  • Community partners, friend groups, faith communities, and business networks willing to use their social capital
